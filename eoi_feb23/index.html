Why do we see the sky blue during the day?

The sunlight is scattered by the atmosphere

Rayleigh scattering

  • Rayleigh scattering explains why the sky appears blue due to light scattering by small particles.
  • The intensity of scattering depends on particle size and light wavelength, described by Rayleigh's law.
  • Blue light scatters more than red light, making the sky blue.

* Rayleigh's theory is a useful approximation despite predating quantum mechanics.
